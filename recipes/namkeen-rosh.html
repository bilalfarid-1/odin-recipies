Namkeen Rosh

Description

Namkeen Rosh, also known as Namkeen Gosht, is a traditional Pakistani dish featuring slow-cooked mutton infused with aromatic spices and a touch of saltiness. This flavorful dish, hailing from the regions of Khyber Pakhtunkhwa and Balochistan, is a must-try for meat lovers seeking a unique culinary adventure.

Ingredients

  • Bakray ka gosht (Mutton) 2 Kg or as required cut into big pieces
  • Charbi (fat of meat) 150 grams
  • Namak (Salt) 2 teaspoons
  • Dar cheeni (Cinnamon) 3 pieces
  • Sabit Kali mirch (Black pepper corns) 10-15
  • Tamatar (Tomato) 2 we need to use these without cutting
  • Piyaz (Onion) 1 large peel it but do not cut it
  • Adrak (Ginger) 1 Inch piece peeled
  • Hari Mirchain (Green Chillies) 2-3

Steps

    1. Wash the meat thoroughly under running water. Pat it dry with paper towels.
    2. Rub the salt evenly over the surface of the meat using your hands. Ensure all sides are well-coated.
    3. Cut the charbi (fat of meat) into small pieces.
    4. Place the charbi in the bottom of a large pot or Dutch oven you will be using to cook the rosh.
    5. Arrange the meat pieces in a single layer on top of the charbi.
    6. Add the whole tomatoes and whole onion to the pot, along with the dar chini (cinnamon sticks), whole ginger (adrak), and whole black peppercorns (sabit kali mirch).
    7. Cover the pot tightly with a lid.
    8. Place a heavy weight on top of the lid to create pressure and prevent steam from escaping.
    9. Cook the meat on low heat for 2 hours, checking on it after 1 hour.
    10. Add water only if necessary, if you feel the meat is drying out before fully cooked.
    11. After 2-2.5 hours, or when the meat is completely tender, add the whole green chilies (sabut hari mirch) to the pot.
    12. Re-cover the pot and cook for an additional 10 minutes to allow the aroma of the green chilies to infuse the rosh.
    13. Your Quetta rosh is now ready to serve alongside naan. Enjoy!
    14. **Note:** Adjust the salt quantity to your preference, but avoid adding chilies as this recipe is traditionally not spicy.
